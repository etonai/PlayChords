# Comedy Song Type 1 Addendum - Chorus Resolution Progressions

## Purpose

This addendum extends the Comedy Song Type 1 design by introducing Chorus Resolution Progressions.

A Chorus Resolution Progression is a special progression generated by the application and intended to replace the final line of a Chorus.

Its purpose is to:

* Provide a stronger ending
* Emphasize a punchline
* Signal the conclusion of a Chorus
* Support audience recognition of a musical ending

## Design Philosophy

Most Chorus lines use the standard Chorus Progression.

The final Chorus line may optionally use the generated Chorus Resolution Progression instead.

The application always generates a Chorus Resolution Progression.

The performer decides whether to use it.

## Requirements

All Chorus Resolution Progressions:

* Must end on I
* Should arrive at I during the final one or two chord positions
* Should feel more final than the standard Chorus Progression
* Are intended only for the final line of a Chorus
* Must contain four chord positions so they can easily replace a normal Chorus line

## Chorus Resolution Progression Pool

### Strong Resolution

* ii - V - I - I

### Broadway Resolution

* IV - V - I - I

### Build and Resolve

* vi - IV - V - I

### Plagal Resolution

* IV - I - I - I

### Dominant Arrival

* V - V - I - I

### Descending Resolution

* I - vi - V - I

### Predominant Resolution

* ii - IV - V - I

### Tonic Emphasis

* I - V - I - I

### Lift and Resolve

* iii - vi - V - I

### Extended Build

* vi - ii - V - I

## Generated Output

The application generates:

* Chorus Progression
* Verse Progression
* Bridge Progression (Optional)
* Modulated Chorus (Optional)
* Chorus Resolution Progression

## Performance Usage

The performer may choose whether to use the Chorus Resolution Progression.

Typical examples:

### Standard Chorus

Line 1:
I - V - vi - IV

Line 2:
I - V - vi - IV

Line 3:
I - V - vi - IV

Line 4:
I - V - vi - IV

### Chorus With Resolution

Line 1:
I - V - vi - IV

Line 2:
I - V - vi - IV

Line 3:
I - V - vi - IV

Line 4:
ii - V - I - I

## Relationship to Modulated Chorus

If a Modulated Chorus is used:

* The Chorus progression is transposed up one whole step.
* The Chorus Resolution Progression is also transposed up one whole step.
* The performer may optionally use the Chorus Resolution Progression during the Modulated Chorus.

Example:

Original key: C major

Chorus:
C - G - Am - F

Resolution:
Dm - G - C - C

Modulated Chorus:
D - A - Bm - G

Modulated Resolution:
Em - A - D - D

## Design Principle

The application should always generate a Chorus Resolution Progression whenever it generates a Chorus.

The performer is free to ignore it.

However, having a generated resolution available gives the performer an immediate mechanism for:

* Ending a Chorus
* Ending a Song
* Supporting a punchline
* Supporting a Modulated Chorus ending
