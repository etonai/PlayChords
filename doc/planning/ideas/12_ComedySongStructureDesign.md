# Improvised Showtune Generator - Comedy Song Type 1

## Style Assumption

This version assumes a broad improvised comedy song rather than a traditional patter song.

Inspirations include:

* Improvised musical theatre songs
* Comedy cabaret songs
* Humorous character songs
* List songs with repeated hooks

The design intentionally favors:

* Clear audience recognition
* Repetition of a memorable chorus
* Flexible song length
* Joke accumulation and escalation
* Strong comedic endings

It does not attempt to emulate rapid-fire patter songs such as The Modern Major-General.

## Goal

Generate a comedy song suitable for improvised musical theatre performance.

The generated song consists of:

1. Chorus
2. Verse
3. Bridge (Optional)
4. Modulated Chorus (Optional)

The resulting progression sequence becomes the harmonic roadmap for the improvised song.

## Emotional Structure

### Chorus

Introduce the premise and hook.

The audience should quickly understand what the song is about.

Examples:

* My roommate is the worst.
* Reasons I should be king.
* Why being a pirate is harder than expected.
* Everything wrong with this kingdom.

The Chorus should be memorable and easy to repeat.

### Verse

The primary joke-generation section.

The performer supplies examples that support the premise established by the Chorus.

The performer may remain in this section for as long as desired.

### Bridge (Optional)

A temporary change of perspective.

Possible uses:

* Escalation
* Unexpected twist
* Different viewpoint
* Callback setup

The Bridge should feel different from the Verse while remaining compatible with the overall song.

### Modulated Chorus (Optional)

A larger and more energetic version of the Chorus.

The Modulated Chorus uses the same progression as the Chorus, transposed up one whole step.

The performer may choose whether to use this section.

## Section Identity Rules

Unlike an I Want or I Love song, this song type is cyclic rather than linear.

The Chorus functions as the anchor of the song.

The Verse functions as a space for generating comedic material.

### Chorus Rules

The Chorus should be:

* Memorable
* Repeatable
* Easy for an audience to recognize

The Chorus progression should support multiple repetitions throughout the song.

### Verse Rules

The Verse should be:

* Harmonically simple
* Easy to improvise over
* Suitable for extended joke delivery

The performer may remain in the Verse for as many examples as desired.

### Bridge Rules

The Bridge should provide contrast.

The Bridge is optional.

If present, it should set up a stronger final Chorus.

### Modulated Chorus Rules

The application always generates a Modulated Chorus.

The Modulated Chorus uses the same progression selected for the Chorus.

The progression is automatically transposed up one whole step.

The performer may choose whether to use the Modulated Chorus during performance.

The Modulated Chorus is intended to provide the biggest ending available within this song type.

## Progression Pools

### Chorus Progressions

* I - V - vi - IV
* I - vi - IV - V
* I - IV - V - I
* IV - V - I - vi
* I - IV - I - V
* vi - IV - I - V

### Verse Progressions

* I - IV - V
* I - IV
* I - V
* I - vi - IV - V
* I - IV - I - V
* I - V - I - IV

### Bridge Progressions

* vi - IV - I - V
* I - IV - vi - V
* I - iii - IV - V
* iii - vi - ii - V
* vi - ii - IV - V
* I - IV - I - ii - V

## Generated Output

The application generates:

* Chorus progression
* Verse progression
* Bridge progression (optional)
* Modulated Chorus progression

The Modulated Chorus progression is generated by transposing the Chorus progression up one whole step.

## Performance Form

The performer determines how many times to cycle between sections.

Typical forms include:

Short Form:

Chorus → Verse → Chorus

Medium Form:

Chorus → Verse → Chorus → Verse → Chorus

Bridge Form:

Chorus → Verse → Chorus → Bridge → Chorus

Extended Form:

Chorus → Verse → Chorus → Verse → Chorus → Bridge → Chorus

Big Finish Form:

Chorus → Verse → Chorus → Bridge → Modulated Chorus

## Modulation Rule

If the Modulated Chorus is performed:

* Repeat the Chorus progression.
* Transpose it up one whole step.
* Remain in the new key through the ending.

Examples:

* C major → D major
* D major → E major
* F major → G major
* Bb major → C major

## Example Output

Selected key: C major

Chorus:

C - G - Am - F

Verse:

C - F - G

Bridge:

Am - F - C - G

Modulated Chorus:

D - A - Bm - G

## Version 1 Constraints

No modulation except the generated Modulated Chorus.

All generated progressions remain in the selected key.

Use major keys only.

The performer controls song length by repeating Verse and Chorus sections as desired.

The Bridge is optional.

The Modulated Chorus is always generated.

The performer decides whether to use the Modulated Chorus.

## Version 1 Design Principle

The app should provide a reliable framework for generating jokes rather than a detailed dramatic journey.

Chorus: establish premise

Verse: generate jokes

Bridge: escalate or redirect

Modulated Chorus: biggest finish

The Chorus is the most important section of the song and should serve as the audience's primary point of recognition throughout the performance.
