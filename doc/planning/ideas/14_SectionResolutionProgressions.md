# Section Resolution Progressions

## Purpose

This document introduces Section Resolution Progressions.

A Section Resolution Progression is a special progression generated by the application and intended to replace the final line of a section.

Its purpose is to:

* Signal the end of a section
* Support transitions between sections
* Support punchlines and important lyrics
* Provide stronger musical direction than the section's normal progression
* Provide a clear musical ending when desired

## Background

Section Resolution Progressions evolved from the Chorus Resolution Progression system originally created for Comedy Song Type 1.

The original Chorus Resolution system generated a progression intended to replace the final line of a Chorus.

This proved useful because:

* It created stronger musical endings.
* It supported punchlines.
* It provided a natural transition point.
* It gave the performer an optional tool for emphasizing important moments.

During review, it became apparent that the same concept could be applied to other sections.

For example:

* A Verse may benefit from a special final line before returning to a Chorus.
* A Chorus may benefit from a stronger ending before a Bridge.
* A Modulated Chorus may benefit from a stronger ending before the conclusion of the song.

As a result, the Chorus Resolution concept was generalized into the Section Resolution system.

Section Resolution Progressions retain the original design goals of Chorus Resolution while becoming reusable across multiple section types and multiple song templates.

## Design Philosophy

Most lines within a section use the section's primary progression.

The final line of a section may optionally use a Section Resolution Progression instead.

The application always generates Section Resolution Progressions for supported sections.

The performer decides whether to use them.

## Requirements

All Section Resolution Progressions:

* Must end on I
* Should arrive at I during the final one or two chord positions
* Should feel more final than the section's normal progression
* Must be suitable for replacing the final line of a section

## Section Resolution Progression Pool

### Strong Resolution

* ii - V - I - I

### Broadway Resolution

* IV - V - I - I

### Build and Resolve

* vi - IV - V - I

### Plagal Resolution

* IV - I - I - I

### Dominant Arrival

* V - V - I - I

### Descending Resolution

* I - vi - V - I

### Predominant Resolution

* ii - IV - V - I

### Tonic Emphasis

* I - V - I - I

### Lift and Resolve

* iii - vi - V - I

### Extended Build

* vi - ii - V - I

## Comedy Song Type 1 Integration

Comedy Song Type 1 generates:

* Chorus Progression
* Chorus Resolution
* Verse Progression
* Verse Resolution
* Bridge Progression (Optional)
* Modulated Chorus Progression (Optional)
* Modulated Chorus Resolution (Optional)

### Chorus Resolution

The Chorus Resolution may replace the final line of a Chorus.

Example:

Line 1:
C - G - Am - F

Line 2:
C - G - Am - F

Line 3:
C - G - Am - F

Line 4:
Dm - G - C - C

### Verse Resolution

The Verse Resolution may replace the final line of a Verse.

The Verse Resolution is intended to support a return to the Chorus or a transition to the Bridge.

Example:

Line 1:
C - F - G

Line 2:
C - F - G

Line 3:
C - F - G

Line 4:
F - G - C - C

The application may select the same Section Resolution Progression for both Chorus and Verse, or different Section Resolution Progressions for each.

## Modulated Chorus Integration

If a Modulated Chorus is generated:

* The Chorus Progression is transposed up one whole step.
* The Chorus Resolution is transposed up one whole step.

The application automatically generates:

* Modulated Chorus Progression
* Modulated Chorus Resolution

The Modulated Chorus Resolution is not independently selected.

It is derived from the Chorus Resolution.

Example:

Original key: C major

Chorus:
C - G - Am - F

Chorus Resolution:
Dm - G - C - C

Modulated Chorus:
D - A - Bm - G

Modulated Chorus Resolution:
Em - A - D - D

## Future Expansion

Section Resolution Progressions are intended to be reusable across multiple song types.

Potential future uses include:

* I Want Song Type 2
* I Love Song Type 2
* Celebration Songs
* Finale Songs
* Villain Songs

This system provides a common mechanism for handling section endings throughout the application.
