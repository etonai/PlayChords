Great. Now that you have an **I Want Type 1**, I'd resist the urge to immediately create dozens of song types.

I'd look for the major recurring dramatic functions that show up in musical theatre and improv shows.

A first pass might be:

### Type 1: I Want Song

A character expresses a dream, desire, or goal.

Examples:

* *Part of Your World*
* *The Wizard and I*
* *Corner of the Sky*

---

### Type 2: Love Song

A character expresses affection, attraction, or devotion.

Examples:

* *Some Enchanted Evening*
* *All I Ask of You*

---

### Type 3: Decision Song

A character must choose between alternatives.

Examples:

* *On My Own*
* *Maybe This Time* (arguably)

These often have more harmonic tension because the character is conflicted.

---

### Type 4: Villain Song

A character explains a philosophy, scheme, or worldview.

Examples:

* *Be Prepared*
* *Hellfire*
* *Dentist!*

These often enjoy stronger dominant motion and more dramatic harmonic shifts.

---

### Type 5: Comedy/List Song

The goal is jokes rather than emotional development.

Examples:

* *A Musical*
* *The Song That Goes Like This*
* *Modern Major General*

The progression can often stay simple because the lyrics are doing the heavy lifting.

---

### Type 6: Celebration Song

The characters are already happy and are enjoying a success.

Examples:

* *Seize the Day*
* *You Can't Stop the Beat*

Very different from an I Want song because the emotional destination has already been reached.

---

### Type 7: Reassurance / Advice Song

One character helps another.

Examples:

* *Hakuna Matata*
* *No One Is Alone*
* *You'll Be Back* (sort of, though twisted)

---

### Type 8: Group Agreement Song

Several characters unite around a common purpose.

Examples:

* *One Day More* (partially)
* *Tonight Quintet* (partially)
* Many act-one finales

---

If this is for an improv-song generator, I'd actually think less about Broadway categories and more about **dramatic functions**.

A lot of songs can be reduced to:

1. I Want
2. I Love
3. I Decide
4. I Explain
5. We Agree
6. We Celebrate
7. I Complain
8. I Scheme

Those are the kinds of prompts an improviser can instantly understand and perform.
