# Improvised Showtune Generator - I Love Song Type 1

## Style Assumption

This version assumes a classic Broadway / American Songbook / standards flavor rather than a modern pop-musical-theatre love song.

Inspirations include:

* Some Enchanted Evening
* If I Loved You
* People Will Say We're in Love
* Younger Than Springtime

The design intentionally favors:

* Stable harmony
* Repetition of harmonic material
* Strong tonic–predominant–dominant relationships
* Emotional warmth rather than dramatic escalation

It does not attempt to emulate contemporary musical theatre styles influenced by pop, rock, or singer-songwriter traditions.

## Goal

Generate a simple four-section "I Love" song suitable for improvised musical theatre performance.

The generated song consists of:

1. Opening
2. Main Love Theme
3. Variant Love Theme (Optional)
4. Climax

The user selects a key, or the app selects a random key. The application chooses one progression from each section's progression pool.

The resulting progression sequence becomes the harmonic roadmap for the improvised song.

## Emotional Structure

### Opening

Introduce the feeling.

The singer observes, reflects, or describes the beloved.

### Main Love Theme

The central statement of affection.

This section contains the primary emotional and harmonic material of the song.

### Variant Love Theme (Optional)

A variation on the central feeling.

This section may provide additional examples, memories, observations, or reflections about the beloved.

The performer may choose to use this section or repeat the Main Love Theme instead.

### Climax

Emotional arrival.

The singer commits emotionally or summarizes the feeling.

## Section Identity Rules

Unlike an I Want song, an I Love song is not primarily about change.

The harmonic language should emphasize stability, warmth, and familiarity.

Repetition of harmonic material is encouraged.

### Opening Rules

The Opening should feel relaxed and reflective.

Allowed emphasis:

* I
* vi
* IV

Avoid strong cadences unless necessary.

### Main Love Theme Rules

The Main Love Theme introduces the primary harmonic movement of the song.

Required tendency:

* Include ii and/or V

The progression should support lyrical development without excessive tension.

### Variant Love Theme Rules

The Variant Love Theme should feel related to the Main Love Theme while providing modest contrast.

Required tendencies:

* Include ii and/or V
* Remain stylistically compatible with the Main Love Theme

Selection rule:

* Must not use the same progression selected for the Main Love Theme

Performance rule:

* The performer may skip this section and repeat the Main Love Theme instead.

### Climax Rules

The Climax should provide a satisfying arrival.

Required tendency:

* Must end on I

Preferred cadences:

* ii - V - I
* IV - V - I

## Progression Pools

### Opening Progressions

* I - vi
* I - IV
* I - vi - IV
* I - IV - I
* I - iii - vi
* I - vi - ii

### Main Love Theme Progressions

* I - vi - ii - V
* I - vi - IV - V
* I - IV - ii - V
* I - vi - I - V
* I - iii - vi - ii - V
* vi - ii - V - I

### Variant Love Theme Progressions

* I - IV - I - ii - V
* vi - IV - I - V
* I - iii - IV - V
* iii - vi - ii - V
* I - IV - vi - V
* vi - ii - IV - V

Selection Rule:

* The Variant Love Theme progression must be different from the Main Love Theme progression.

### Climax Progressions

* ii - V - I
* IV - V - I
* I - vi - ii - V - I
* IV - I - ii - V - I
* ii - IV - V - I
* IV - V - I - vi - ii - V - I

## Example Output

Selected key: C major

Opening:

C - Am

Main Love Theme:

C - Am - Dm - G

Variant Love Theme:

Am - F - C - G

Climax:

Dm - G - C

## Version 1 Constraints

No modulation.

All progressions remain in the selected key.

Use major keys only.

Each section uses one selected progression.

The pianist may loop each section as long as needed before moving on.

The Variant Love Theme section is optional.

## Version 1 Design Principle

The app should generate a stable emotional experience rather than a dramatic journey.

Opening: reflective

Main Love Theme: affectionate

Variant Love Theme: expanded reflection

Climax: resolved

The harmonic center of gravity for this song type is:

I - vi - ii - V

Many generated songs should naturally gravitate toward this progression, giving the resulting songs a classic Broadway and American Songbook character.
